Teriyaki Salmon

Teriyaki salmon's background originates from the Japanese Edo period (1603-1868) as a cooking technique called teriyaki, which combines "teri" (shine) and "yaki" (grill). Originally, it was a subtle method of grilling fish with a glaze of soy sauce, sake, and sugar for flavor and preservation. This evolved into the Americanized versions we know today, often sweeter and sometimes incorporating ingredients like pineapple juice in Hawaii or becoming a fast-food staple in cities like Seattle.

More Info

This is an easy salmon dinner that the whole family will love. My mom made it herself and it is big on Asian flavors :).The fish is typically pan-seared, baked, or broiled to perfection, resulting in a flaky interior and a beautifully glazed, sometimes crispy, exterior. The sauce is made from simple ingredients such as soy sauce, mirin (sweet rice wine), sake, and sugar, often enhanced with ginger and garlic.

Main Ingredients

  • Salmon fillets: 4 to 6 (about 6 ounces each)
  • Soy sauce: \(1/4\) to \(1/3\) cup, or to taste
  • Honey or brown sugar: \(1.5\) to \(2\) tablespoons, or to taste
  • Garlic: 1-2 cloves, minced or grated, or \(1/2\) teaspoon garlic powder
  • Ginger: \(1\) teaspoon, freshly grated or minced

Optional Ingredients

  1. Mirin: \(1/3\) cup (sweet rice wine)
  2. Sake: 2 tablespoons
  3. Sriracha: 1 tablespoon
  4. Apple cider vinegar: \(1.5\) tablespoonsM
  5. Cornstarch: 1 teaspoon (to thicken sauce)
  6. Olive oil: 2 tablespoons
